Mary Donne Peter, J.D.

Senior Associate

Mary Donne Peters represents high growth companies—including government contractors— often serving as outside general counsel. She has handled litigation matters in over thirty states. Before entering private practice, Mary Donne served as an Assistant U.S. Attorney for the District of South Carolina and an Honors Attorney for the Antitrust Division of the U.S. Justice Department. Mary Donne began her career in private practice with a large national law firm in Atlanta but has spent the last two decades in a law firm that she co-founded with her partner Mike Gorby. Mary Donne is a frequent lecturer for national legal and business groups and the author of a book published by the world’s largest legal publisher. A passionate entrepreneur herself, Mary Donne is a member of the Women President’s Organization, a non-profit organization for women presidents, CEOs and managing directors of privately held, multimillion dollar companies.


Mary Donne holds licenses to practice law in Georgia, Alabama and South Carolina. She is rated AV-Preeminent® (the highest level of professional excellence) by the Lexis-Nexis Peer Review Ratings™ System and has been selected as a Super Lawyer® by the Thomson Reuters® peer review system.

Mike Gorby, J.D.

Senior Associate

Mike Gorby is a founding member of Gorby Peters & Associates, LLC. He is a nationally recognized leader in complex corporate disputes, and liability issues that arise for property owners and managers, such as hotel chains, restaurants, shopping centers, apartment complexes, office buildings, and other public venues.


Mike’s work also includes advising start up clients on the different forms of business structures and the most advantageous entity selection for the client. Mike has served as the program chair for the Institute of Continuing Legal Education in Georgia (an affiliate of the University of Georgia) on the topic of Premises Liability. He is also the author of a popular legal treatise, Premises Liability in Georgia, which is published by Thomson Reuters/West, and which is now in its Third edition. Mike has served as an instructor/lecturer for Georgia Tech Procurement Assistance Center (GTPAC) in the area of small business procurement of government contracts. Mike is also a registered mediator. Mike is a veteran of the United States Army..

Alexis Kirksey, B.S., M.S., Accounting

Senior Associate

Alexis Kirksey’s expertise includes providing consultation to government and industry in all aspects of government contracting, including market research, solicitation and proposal preparation, contract accounting, contract administration, small business certifications, and Defense Contract Audit Agency (DCAA)/ Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) compliance. Ms. Kirksey has created and delivered custom curriculum to over 20 colleges and universities, as well as local, state, and federal government agencies including the Department of Defense and the State of Georgia. Ms. Kirksey served as the Program Manager of The Contracting Education Academy at Georgia Tech from 2018 – 2021, where she was responsible for teaching and designing instructional materials for all stakeholders in the government contracting arena on a variety of topics. She has served as a Defense Acquisition University (DAU) equivalency instructor since 2015.


Alexis has an M.S. and B.S. in Accounting from Kennesaw State University.

Tom Larkin, B.S. Animal Science, M.S. Management

Senior Associate

A 20-year veteran of the United States Army, Tom Larkin has over 35 years’ experience in planning, executing, and administering government contracts in variety of positions as Contracting Officer and Administrative Contracting Officer. At the Tank and Automotive Command, he served as a contract specialist and Procuring Contracting Officer purchasing spare parts and tank engines with an annual obligation of over $68M. As the Administrative Contracting Officer at the Detroit Tank Arsenal (Tank Production Facility) he oversaw the $2.1 Billion production contract for the M1Abrams Main Battle Tank. Moving from “practitioner” to Educator, Tom became a faculty member as an Assistant Professor of Contracting at the Air Force Institute of Technology instructing Government employees how to perform purchasing functions. He later participated in the startup and development of Defense Acquisition University.


Later in his career, he completed 10 years of service with the Georgia Tech Procurement Technical Assistance Center (GTPAC) as an educator and business counselor for over 4500 Georgia businesses concerning how to navigate the bidding process in providing goods and services to local, State, and Federal Agencies or municipalities. Businesses receiving this assistance received as a Prime Vendor or Subcontractor over $550M in contract awards.
